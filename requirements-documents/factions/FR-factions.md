# Tag Teams, Factions & Relationships - Executive Summary

Wrestling thrives on gangs, stables, tag teams, and blood feuds. This feature introduces the logic to group wrestlers into Factions, manage deep interpersonal relationships (Friendships and Rivalries), and simulate 2v2 and 3v3 team matches.

## High-Level Requirements (HLR) Document

**Feature ID**: F-006
**Feature Name**: Factions, Relationships & Team Simulation
**Description**: Introduce a `Faction` model and a complex `Relationship Engine`. Wrestlers will form Friendships and Rivalries based on their Alignment (Face/Heel), Ego, and In-Ring Styles. Factions gain Popularity, but overly dominant factions suffer a "bloat penalty." The Match Simulator will be updated to handle multi-man team matches (2v2, 3v3) and apply massive hype boosts when bitter rivals face each other.

**User Stories**:
- As a Booker, I want my wrestlers to naturally form Friendships or Rivalries based on their personality (Ego, Professionalism) and alignment (Faces hate Heels).
- As a Booker, I want Faces and Heels to be strictly barred from joining the same Faction or being kayfabe friends.
- As a Booker, I want highly hyped factions to attract high-hype wrestlers to join them, assuming they don't have clashing Egos.
- As a Booker, I want the crowd to turn on massively bloated factions, applying a negative penalty to the individual members' Hype/Heat/Pop.
- As a Booker, I want to book 2v2 and 3v3 matches on my `BookingSheet`.
- As a Referee (Match Engine), I need to simulate team matches and inject massive crowd excitement boosts when bitter rivals share the ring.

**Functional Requirements**:
- **FR-6.1.0 - The Faction Model**: Create `Faction` model containing `name`, `alignment`, `leader_id`, `members` (list of UUIDs), and `popularity`.
- **FR-6.2.0 - Wrestler Relationships**: Add `friendships` and `rivalries` dictionaries to the `Wrestler` model (`Dict[UUID, int]`). Add an optional `faction_id`.
- **FR-6.3.0 - Relationship Engine**: Create `relationship_engine.py` to evaluate chemistry between two wrestlers:
  - High Ego clashes with High Ego (increases Rivalry).
  - Faces and Heels have an inherent Rivalry baseline; they cannot be Friends.
  - Similar high professionalism/work rate generates respect (Friendship).
- **FR-6.4.0 - Faction Dynamics Engine**: Determine if a wrestler can join a faction.
  - Requirement: Must match Faction Alignment. Must have high friendship with the leader OR massive personal hype (with low enough Ego to submit to a leader).
- **FR-6.5.0 - Faction Bloat Penalty**: If a faction exceeds 4 members, a negative modifier is applied to the individual members' popularity calculations.
- **FR-6.6.0 - Team Match Booking**: Update `BookingSheet`. Replace the flat `participants` list with `teams` (e.g., `List[List[UUID]]`).
- **FR-6.7.0 - Rivalry Match Boost**: In `match_simulator.py`, if opposing team members have a high Rivalry score, apply a multiplier to the heat generated by their moves.

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- A Face and a Heel cannot have a positive Friendship score and cannot join the same Faction.
- Two 100-Ego Heels will quickly generate a Rivalry score rather than becoming friends.
- A 2v2 tag match simulates cleanly, alternating turns between Team A and Team B.
- A match between two bitter rivals generates significantly more star rating/heat than the exact same match between two strangers.

## Logic Blueprint

- **Relationship Chemistry**: `Chemistry = (Similiarity in Prof/WorkRate) - (Combined Ego Penalty) - (Alignment Clash Penalty)`. Positive = Friendship, Negative = Rivalry.
- **Faction Joining**: `can_join = True IF (Alignments Match) AND ((friendship[leader] > 70) OR (wrestler.hype > 80 AND wrestler.ego < 80))`
- **Bloat Penalty**: `penalty = (len(members) - 4) * 10%`. Applied as a reduction to individual hype.
